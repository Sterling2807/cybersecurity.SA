Cybersecurity Objectives

Cybersecurity must establish rules that minimize the risks to the information or computer infrastructure. These rules include operating hours, restrictions to certain places, authorizations, denials, user profiles, emergency plans, protocols and everything necessary to allow a good level of computer security, minimizing the impact on the performance of workers and the organization. in general and as the main contributor to the use of programs made by programmers.

Cybersecurity is designed to protect computing assets, including the following:

The computational infrastructure:

it is a fundamental part for the storage and management of the information, as well as for the very operation of the organization. The role of computer security in this area is to ensure that equipment works properly and to anticipate in the event of failures, thefts, fires, sabotage, natural disasters, power failures and any other factor that threatens the computer infrastructure.

Users:

they are the people who use the technological structure, the communications area and who manage the information. The system in general must be protected so that their use cannot jeopardize the security of the information, nor that the information they handle or store is vulnerable.

The information:

this is the main asset. It uses and resides in the computing infrastructure and is used by the users.
